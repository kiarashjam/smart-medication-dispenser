## Slide 5: How It Works

**HOW IT WORKS**  
From Setup to Peace of Mind in 5 Steps

1. **Setup** — Caregiver configures schedule once via web portal  
2. **Dispense** — Device releases correct pills at the right time  
3. **Notify** — App sends alert + device sounds reminder  
4. **Confirm** — Patient removes pills; sensors verify  
5. **Report** — Caregiver sees "dose confirmed" in real time  

**If missed — Smart Escalation Protocol:**

| 0 min | 15 min | 30 min | 60 min |
|:------|:-------|:-------|:-------|
| Device alarm + screen | App push reminder | Caregiver alert via app | Emergency contact notified |

**Speaker Notes:**

> Here's the user experience in five steps. First, the caregiver — say Sophie — opens our web portal and sets up her mother's medication schedule. She does this once. From that point, everything is automatic. At the scheduled time, the device rotates to the correct compartment and releases the pills into a tray. Simultaneously, the patient gets a reminder on the device screen, through the speaker, and on their phone. The patient takes their pills, and our optical and weight sensors verify the tray has been emptied. Sophie instantly sees "dose confirmed" on her phone. But the real magic is what happens when something goes wrong. If the pills aren't taken, we don't just send one notification and give up. We have a four-stage escalation protocol: device alarm, then app push notification, then caregiver alert, and finally emergency contact notification at 60 minutes. This is how we prevent the missed doses that lead to hospitalizations.
