# Go-to-Market Strategy

**Launch & Growth Plan**

**Lausanne, Switzerland First**

---

## Executive Summary

Our go-to-market strategy follows a disciplined geographic expansion with Switzerland as the launch market, followed by DACH expansion in Year 2, and broader European markets in Year 3+.

| Phase | Timeline | Markets | Customer Target |
|:------|:---------|:--------|----------------:|
| **Launch** | Months 1-6 | Lausanne/Vaud | 100 |
| **Swiss Expansion** | Months 7-12 | All Switzerland | 400 |
| **DACH** | Months 13-24 | Germany, Austria | 2,000 |
| **Europe** | Months 25-36 | France, Benelux | 6,000 |

**Year 1 Goals:** 550 customers, CHF 150K revenue, 3-5 pharmacy partnerships

## 1. Market Entry Strategy

### 1.1 Why Switzerland First?

| Factor | Benefit | Data |
|:-------|:--------|:-----|
| **Home base** | Local support, fast iteration | HQ in Lausanne |
| **High ARPU** | Premium pricing acceptable | CHF 35-50/mo vs. €25-35 EU |
| **Wealthy population** | Willingness to pay | GDP per capita CHF 92K |
| **Quality expectations** | "Swiss made" premium | Brand alignment |
| **Small market** | Manageable test | 8.8M population |
| **Languages** | Test all EU languages | FR/DE/IT |
| **Regulatory** | MRA with EU | CE = Swiss market |

### 1.2 Why Lausanne Specifically?

| Factor | Advantage |
|:-------|:----------|
| **Headquarters** | Local team can provide hands-on support |
| **EPFL/UNIL** | Tech-savvy population, early adopters |
| **CHUV** | Major hospital for B2B pilot |
| **Vaud demographics** | 19% 65+, 170K seniors in canton |
| **Francophone** | Test French messaging before France |
| **Affluent** | High disposable income |
| **Size** | 350K metro area — manageable for launch |

### 1.3 Geographic Expansion Sequence

| Priority | Market | Entry | Rationale |
|:---------|:-------|:------|:----------|
| 1 | **Vaud/Lausanne** | Month 1 | Home base |

## 6. Sales Strategy

### 6.1 B2C Sales Process

| Stage | Channel | Action | Conversion Target |
|:------|:--------|:-------|:------------------|
| Awareness | Ads, PR | See message | — |
| Interest | Website | Visit, read | 5% of visitors |
| Consideration | Demo video | Watch demo | 15% of interested |
| Trial | Contact | Book call | 20% of consideration |
| Purchase | Call/online | Complete order | 40% of trials |
| Onboarding | Support | Setup complete | 95% |
| Retention | Success | Month 6+ active | 85% |

### 6.2 B2B Sales Process

| Stage | Activities | Duration | Owner |
|:------|:-----------|:---------|:------|
| **Prospecting** | Research, outreach | Ongoing | Sales |
| **Qualification** | Discovery call | 1 week | Sales |
| **Demo** | Product demonstration | 1-2 weeks | Sales |
| **Proposal** | Custom proposal | 1 week | Sales |
| **Pilot** | Trial with 5-10 patients | 1-2 months | Success |
| **Negotiation** | Contract terms | 2-4 weeks | Sales |
| **Close** | Signature | 1 week | Sales |
| **Onboarding** | Implementation | 2-4 weeks | Success |

### 6.3 B2B Sales Playbook: Pharmacies

**Week 1-2: Prospecting**
- Identify target pharmacies (Amavita, SunStore, independents)
- Research pharmacy, owner, patient demographics
- Find warm introduction (if possible)
- Send personalized outreach email

**Week 2-4: Qualification**
- Discovery call (20 minutes)
- Understand current patient services
- Identify pain points (competition from online pharmacies)
- Qualify: budget, decision process, timeline

**Week 4-6: Demo**
- In-pharmacy demonstration
- Show device, app, pharmacy dashboard
- Calculate potential patient volume
- Present ROI model

**Week 6-8: Pilot**
- Free 30-day pilot with 5-10 patients
- Weekly check-in calls
- Gather feedback and testimonials
- Demonstrate results

**Week 8-12: Close**
- Present pilot results
- Propose rollout plan
- Negotiate terms
- Sign agreement
